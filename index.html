Who am I?

Even thought I took computer science for a year at my high school, I never started coding until I got to college. At University at Buffalo, I was fascinated by the world of computer science and I immediately found my passion in programming. So when I switched my major from Mechanical Engineering to Computer Science in my freshman year, my parents were the least surprised because ever since I was young, I loved building and breaking. And to me, the basics of programming has always been about building a great product by breaking down multiple concepts.

Apart from programming, I love music and cooking. Music has always found a way to calm, motivate or entertain me. If you are ever on UB campus, you can always spot me with my big headphones on. Cooking has always been a leisure activity for me. I owe a lot of my creative growth to my midnight cravings.

When I am not doing Computer Science, I am either playing snooker, watching hysterical youtube videos, doing some adventurous activity, usually alone or listening to podcasts about the start-ups world.
